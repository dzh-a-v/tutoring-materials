Concept

The big idea: same angle, same ratios

Why ratios stay constant

In similar triangles, corresponding lengths are multiplied by the same scale factor.

Triangle opp / hyp adj / hyp opp / adj
3, 4, 5 3 / 5 4 / 5 3 / 4
6, 8, 10 6 / 10 8 / 10 6 / 8
9, 12, 15 9 / 15 12 / 15 9 / 12
For a fixed angle θ, the side ratios are invariant: they do not change when the triangle is enlarged.

Vocabulary

Side names depend on the angle

Definitions

Hypotenuse: the longest side, opposite the right angle.

Opposite: the side across from the chosen angle.

Adjacent: the side next to the chosen angle that is not the hypotenuse.

Careful

If the chosen angle changes, opposite and adjacent can swap. The hypotenuse never changes.

Use when adjacent and hypotenuse are involved.

Use when opposite and adjacent are involved.

Memory support

SOH   CAH   TOA

Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse, Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse, Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent.

Meaning

A trigonometric ratio compares two sides in a right-angled triangle for a chosen angle.

Representation

Where do sine and cosine come from?

Core meaning

On a unit circle, the radius is 1. For an angle θ, the point has coordinates:

x = cos θ
y = sin θ

Scaling the radius from 1 to a hypotenuse of h gives:

adjacent = h cos θ
opposite = h sin θ

Extension direction: exact values at 30°, 45°, and 60° can wait until the core ratios are secure.

Worked example

Find a missing length

Method

  1. Known: hypotenuse. Need: opposite.
  2. Choose sine: sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse.
  3. Substitute and solve.
sin 32° = opposite10
opposite = 10 sin 32°
opposite ≈ 5.3 cm

Calculator routine: check degree mode, type the full expression, then round at the end.

Worked example

Find a missing angle

Question

A right-angled triangle has opposite side 8 cm and adjacent side 15 cm. Find angle θ.

Method

Opposite and adjacent are involved, so use tangent.

tan θ = 815
θ = tan-1(8 / 15)
θ ≈ 28.1°

tan-1 means "the angle whose tangent is..." It is the inverse tangent button.

Guided practice

Try, check, improve

1. A triangle has opposite 9, adjacent 40, hypotenuse 41. Write sin θ, cos θ, and tan θ.
2. Hypotenuse = 18 cm and angle = 41°. Find the adjacent side.
3. Opposite = 5 m and adjacent = 12 m. Find angle θ.
4. You stand 9 m from a tree. The angle of elevation to the top is 38°. Estimate the height above eye level.
1. sin θ = 9/41, cos θ = 40/41, tan θ = 9/40.
2. cos 41° = a/18, so a = 18 cos 41° ≈ 13.6 cm.
3. tan θ = 5/12, so θ = tan-1(5/12) ≈ 22.6°.
4. tan 38° = h/9, so h = 9 tan 38° ≈ 7.0 m.

Misconceptions

Common errors

Error 1: side names from the wrong angle

Opposite and adjacent must be labelled from the marked angle, not from the side that looks most important.

Error 2: using Pythagoras for an angle

Pythagoras connects side lengths. Trigonometry connects sides and angles.

Error 3: calculator in radians

For this lesson, angles are in degrees. The calculator should show DEG.

Error 4: rounding too early

Keep the full calculator value until the final answer, then round to the required accuracy.

Check: diagram → labels → ratio → equation → calculator → sensible answer.
